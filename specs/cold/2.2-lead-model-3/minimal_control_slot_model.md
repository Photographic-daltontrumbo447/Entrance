# Minimal Control Slot Model

> Status: pending draft
> Scope: v0 control-slot boundary between `Human / NOTA / Policy / Operation / Execution`

## Purpose

- define the smallest hard control-slot cut that preserves Human sovereignty without collapsing internal strategy and execution roles
- bind control slots to routing and escalation rules rather than to prestige wording
- keep the control surface OS-driven: boundary input enters through governed objects and runtime routing, not courtesy-based edit rights

## Slot Stack

### `Human`

- final sovereignty source
- no direct project-internal canonical write path
- may speak semantically through `NOTA`
- may trigger break-glass runtime control, but runtime writes the resulting admin or integrity records

### `NOTA`

- Human-facing semantic ingress and egress host
- boundary-scoped continuity, clarification, wake, and cadence host
- may author boundary objects such as `INTAKE_BUNDLE` or other NOTA-local boundary records
- not the owner of internal strategy decomposition, implementation review, or foreign-slot truth mutation

### `Policy`

- highest internal strategy writer
- owns strategy-scope semantic truth inside owned scope
- may decide, block, replace, or escalate only through routed objects and runtime-owned control paths

### `Operation`

- middle execution-management slot
- owns preparation, coordination, review, and integration work inside owned scope
- does not redefine top-level policy truth

### `Execution`

- lowest internal production slot
- owns bounded local artifact production and local execution work
- does not choose its own authority boundary or mutate upper-slot truth

## Routing Boundary

### v0 rule

- semantic ingress from Human enters the project only through `NOTA`
- project-internal lineage begins only after runtime admits a NOTA-authored boundary object into internal scope
- default inward routing target is `Policy`; any local NOTA-only closure should remain boundary-local rather than pretending to be project-internal lineage
- internal cross-slot effect travels through routed objects, verdicts, receipts, and runtime-owned queue placement rather than direct foreign-room mutation

### Consequence

- `NOTA` does not collapse into `Policy`
- higher slots remain stronger in authority, but not broader in direct write rights
- control structure compiles as single-writer ownership plus routed effect, not shared mutable hierarchy

## Escalation Boundary

### v0 rule

- `Execution` escalates unresolved non-local blockers upward through the governed exception or return paths
- `Operation` may repair locally, return work, integrate accepted work, or escalate stronger unresolved blockers upward
- `Policy` is the highest internal decision point, but unresolved canonical conflict or Human-needed wake should surface back through `NOTA`
- `NOTA` owns the Human-facing surfacing of interruption, continuity, and wake requests; it does not bypass internal slots to rewrite project truth directly

### Consequence

- escalation remains visible and routed
- Human wake-up stays boundary-hosted by `NOTA`
- no slot needs superuser mutation rights in order to remain operationally strong

## Naming Rule

### v0 rule

- the canonical internal slot names remain `Policy / Operation / Execution`
- `Arch / Dev / Agent` may remain as a SWE-facing projection only when they map back cleanly to those internal slots
- do not reopen `Leader / Manager / Agent` as canonical OS naming

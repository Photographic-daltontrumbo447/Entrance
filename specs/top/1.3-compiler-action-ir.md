## Confirmed Oracle Points

- This document preserves the compiler/runtime hardening cut beneath [machine.md](./machine.md): typed objects, typed codes, routed delivery surfaces, and field ownership.
- The minimal compiler registry cut remains `object_kind / state_code / control_policy`.
- `control_policy` carries writer, route, gate, sandbox, admission, and projection subcodes rather than leaving enforcement in prose or row-local flags.
- Reusable kinds such as `SUBMISSION_PACKET`, `EXCEPTION_PACKET`, `SIMULATION_EVIDENCE`, and `RECEIPT` are not fixed to one slot; effective admissibility comes from policy plus lineage context.
- `simulation_evidence` is a governed object family backed by real receipts and artifacts; minimal admissibility requires both a lineage-linked attempt receipt and an artifact manifest.
- `SUBMISSION_PACKET`, `EXCEPTION_PACKET`, `VERDICT`, and `RECEIPT` remain distinct object classes with distinct writers and semantics.
- Runtime emits `ADMISSION` before an upward packet becomes visible to the receiver queue, and a single `ADMISSION` receipt kind remains sufficient at v0.
- Route target, effective control policy, and return action should be runtime-derived from topology plus registry context rather than trusted from model-authored rows.
- Minimal compiler IR still distinguishes `model-authored / runtime-derived / runtime-only`, with single-writer enforcement on each field class.
- `LEARN_CAPTURE` remains a first-class local learning object at v0, `VERDICT` remains `LOCAL_ONLY`, and runtime-generated evidence should surface through separate runtime facts rather than mixed writer append on one evidence row.
- Engagement presets may tune downstream surfacing or budget behavior, but they do not require a separate compiler-core registry and must not rewrite canonical routing or truth semantics.

## Current Boundary Reading

- the compiler layer should turn role semantics into enforceable transitions and packets, not restate root architecture
- hard routing belongs to compiled control semantics rather than after-the-fact review
- runtime should trust governed evidence objects and receipts rather than self-reported `simulated` claims
- ids, timestamps, queue placement, and delivery edges remain runtime territory
- model slots never write foreign-slot truth directly; cross-slot effect travels through packets, verdicts, receipts, or runtime-owned queue placement
